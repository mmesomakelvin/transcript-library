# Executive summary
A practical 2026 church-camera buying guide organized around *decisions first* (operators, quality vs convenience, live vs recorded), then recommendations by category (PTZ, camcorder, mirrorless, cinema, studio). The through-line: buy what fits your workflow/ecosystem and don’t overbuy bodies you can’t properly support.

# Decision framework (the part worth keeping)
1) **Manned vs unmanned**
- Operators available → camcorders / mirrorless / cinema / studio
- No operators → PTZ

2) **Image quality vs convenience**
- Mirrorless/cinema look better (larger sensors), but require more setup
- Camcorders/PTZ are easier but usually smaller sensors → less image quality

3) **Live production vs recorded content**
- Live: SDI, tally, remote control, integration with switchers
- Recorded: can get away with cards + simpler outputs

# Recommendations by category (as stated)
## PTZ
- Top: **Canon CR‑N300** (~$3.5k) — 20x zoom, auto tracking, color match with Canon line
- Budget: **Canon CR‑N100** (~$2.5k) — similar guts, no SDI (HDMI-only)

**PTZ reality check:** A big chunk of cost is the PTZ mechanism; a $5k PTZ can look like a ~$2.5k camcorder.

# Key takeaways
- **Match your ecosystem**: mixing brands is a color-matching headache.
- **Don’t overbuy**: the best camera is the one you can run *well* (controller, lenses, audio, training).
- **PTZ autotracking ≠ operator** (yet).
- 2026 is “clarity” because gear churn slowed; last year’s good gear is still good.

# Action checklist (for buying)
1) Decide operator reality (manned/unmanned)
2) Decide primary output (live vs recorded)
3) Choose ecosystem anchor (Canon vs Blackmagic vs Sony)
4) Budget for the *system* (controller, lenses, cabling, switcher integration, volunteer training)
5) Only then pick the body
