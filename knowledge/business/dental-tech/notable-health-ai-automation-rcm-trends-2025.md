# AI and Automation in Revenue Cycle Management: Must-Know Trends for 2025

_By Rebecca Harvey, Notable Health_
_Published: June 18, 2025_

## Executive Summary

As 2025 progresses, healthcare organizations face a critical inflection point in revenue cycle management evolution. AI and automation are transitioning from futuristic concepts to operational necessities, with 86% of health systems already leveraging AI. Industry leaders are shifting from workforce expansion strategies to technology-driven transformation, addressing mounting financial pressures, staffing challenges, and patient experience demands.

## Current State of AI Adoption in Healthcare

### Market Penetration

- **86% of health systems** currently leverage AI in their organizations (HIMSS/Medscape Report)
- **72% of executives** prioritize AI/automation for revenue cycle investment in next 12 months
- Applications span from EHR inbox management to AI scribes for documentation

### Mindset Transformation

**Traditional Approach**: "How do we hire more people to do these tasks?"
**Modern Approach**: "How can we leverage technology to change what's possible?"

_Source: Franco Cardillo, Executive Director of Digital Strategy and Operations, MUSC Health_

## Key Industry Challenges Driving AI Adoption

### Financial Pressures

- **$20 billion annually**: Cost of increasing denials to hospitals
- **$25 average cost**: Per denied claim rework expense
- **8.3% year-over-year drop**: Revenue collection rate decline

### Administrative Burden

- **90% of healthcare leaders** report revenue cycle labor challenges exacerbating operations
- Understaffing combined with denial increases creates severe financial impacts
- Manual, repetitive tasks consume valuable staff resources

## Transformative Impact of AI and Automation

### Operational Benefits

1. **Reduced Administrative Costs**
   - Automation of manual, repetitive tasks
   - Decreased errors in billing, coding, and charge capture
   - Elimination of delays from manual processes

2. **Enhanced Revenue Performance**
   - Improved revenue predictability
   - Accelerated reimbursement timelines
   - Better compliance for maximized reimbursements

3. **Strategic Resource Allocation**
   - Staff freed from repetitive tasks
   - Focus shifted to strategy and patient service
   - Time reclaimed for impactful revenue cycle initiatives

### Prime Automation Targets

According to Dayne Hoffman, Revenue Cycle Solutions Lead at Notable:

- Authorizations
- Coding processes
- Claims scrubbing
- Denial management

**Key Principle**: "We aren't looking to replace people, but to unburden them."

## Revenue Cycle as Healthcare Innovation Catalyst

### Paradigm Shift in Innovation

**Historical Pattern**: Breakthroughs from clinical side (new therapies, diagnostics, devices)
**Emerging Reality**: AI's first proving ground in revenue cycle management

"This time, the back office might just be the place that drives healthcare forward."

- _Dayne Hoffman, Notable_

### Strategic Importance

"Everything goes back to the revenue cycle. You can't do anything that we're doing with technology if you don't include the revenue cycle."

- _Franco Cardillo, MUSC Health_

## Payer Dynamics and Financial Strategy

### Increasing Payer Aggression

Matt Morgan, Vice President/CFO of Montage Health, notes:

- "Our team is working harder than ever before to collect the revenues we have earned"
- Payers becoming more aggressive in denying or delaying payments
- Need for proactive documentation management

### Strategic Responses

- Deployment of technology for appeals support
- Prevention of revenue leakage
- Focus on timely, accurate reimbursement

**Core Principle**: "Cash is king" - ensuring financial stability through optimized collections

## Implementation Considerations

### Clinical vs. Administrative AI Deployment

- **Clinical Side**: Rightful caution about untested tools at bedside
- **Administrative Side**: Enormous potential impact on manual, repetitive, under-resourced work
- Lower risk profile for administrative automation enables faster adoption

### Success Factors

1. Leadership commitment to technology transformation
2. Strategic selection of automation targets
3. Focus on staff empowerment vs. replacement
4. Integration with existing revenue cycle workflows

## Future Outlook: 2025 and Beyond

### Near-Term Projections

- Organizations implementing AI for RCM in 2025 will gain competitive advantage
- Early adopters positioning for success in 2026 and beyond
- Momentum building toward standardization of AI in revenue cycles

### Long-Term Implications

- Revenue cycle becoming primary driver of healthcare innovation
- Shift from reactive to proactive financial management
- Integration of AI across entire revenue cycle continuum

## Key Takeaways

1. **AI adoption is accelerating**: From experimental to essential in revenue cycle operations
2. **Administrative focus first**: Lower risk, higher impact area for initial deployment
3. **Staff augmentation, not replacement**: Technology unburdening workers for strategic tasks
4. **Financial imperative**: Addressing $20B denial problem requires technological innovation
5. **Early movers advantage**: 2025 implementations position organizations for future success
