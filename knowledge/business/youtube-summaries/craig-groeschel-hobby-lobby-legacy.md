# Building Hobby Lobby's Legacy—One Leader at a Time | Mart Green

**Channel:** Craig Groeschel | **Words:** 13176

## One-Sentence Summary
Mart Green shares principles of empowerment, generosity, and eternal thinking that built billion dollar family businesses and ministry impact.

## Executive Summary
In this comprehensive leadership conversation, Craig Groeschel interviews Mart Green, founder of Mardell Christian Stores (40+ locations, 800 employees) and ministry investment officer for Hobby Lobby (1,000+ stores). The discussion reveals how intentional relationship documentation, systematic learning from books (over 1,000 books catalogued with favorite quotes), and character-based hiring (generosity, humility, integrity) create sustainable organizational impact. Green's journey began at age 19 when his father helped him secure a loan to start what became Mardell, despite having zero retail experience beyond childhood frame-gluing for Hobby Lobby's startup.

The interview uncovers Green's "transformational unifier" identity—bridging disconnected parties to accomplish together what they couldn't separately, then working himself out once the bridge is built. His approach to people includes documenting first meetings with key facts for future reference, remembering anniversaries and milestones, and giving deeply symbolic gifts that connect to individual stories. Green emphasizes three character traits (GHI: generosity, humility, integrity) as antidotes to Satan's corruption of neutral possessions, power, and pleasure. His stewardship philosophy balances heart (generosity), head (discernment), and Holy Spirit prompting when allocating family wealth toward Bible translation, The Chosen series, and YouVersion Bible app initiatives.

Critical leadership lessons include letting people go faster when you know they're wrong fits (double negative: they can't do the job and aren't moving toward their next opportunity), speaking last to avoid killing ideas with positional authority, and accepting things outside personal preferences as organizations scale. Green's daily disciplines include praying three verses before Bible reading, incorporating 31 "gateways to intimacy" with God, listening to 10 songs daily (curating 12 annual favorites for his kids), and running 30 minutes to care for body/mind/spirit. The family mission statement "to love God intimately and live extravagant generosity" drives his eternal ROI focus—asking "what matters 10,000 years from now?" to filter decisions and investments.
