# 182. Managing Demand, Staffing Shortages & Holding the Line

**Channel:** 3D Dentists | **Words:** 5525

## One-Sentence Summary
Dr. Sully Sullivan addresses hygienist staffing shortages through priority scheduling and holding strategic capacity despite demand pressures.

## Executive Summary
In this Millennial Dentist podcast episode, Dr. Sully Sullivan tackles a universal business problem through the lens of dental practice management: how to handle overwhelming demand when supply is suddenly reduced. After receiving a two-week notice from a hygienist, he faces a one-third reduction in hygiene capacity while demand remains constant or increases. Rather than simply trying to fill the gap or compress existing patients into available slots, Sullivan advocates for "holding the line"—maintaining fundamental business systems like new patient blocks, priority scheduling, and strategic capacity management even when it creates short-term anxiety.

The episode reveals broader principles applicable to any service business: when you say yes to something, you say no to something else; priority scheduling requires leadership courage to leave valuable time open for high-value opportunities; and crisis moments reveal whether you'll abandon core systems or strengthen them. Sullivan shares practical tactics including confirmation protocol enforcement (removing unconfirmed patients at 48 hours), eliminating low-value patient segments (children under certain ages), reducing insurance participation, and implementing pre-appointment fees. His practice achieved $8.2M in revenue in 2025 by setting aggressive short-term goals that kept the team focused, demonstrating that intentional constraint and prioritization drive performance better than trying to serve everyone.

## Practical Recommendations

- Remove unconfirmed patients at forty eight hours and fill slots train patients respect capacity value
- Block new patient slots and hold them despite pressure protects growth lifeblood even during crisis
- Audit which scheduled patients hygienists know will likely cancel stop reappointing them to preserve capacity
- Move minimum patient age up if practice evolved beyond family model formally change who you serve
- Stop automatic six month recalls move healthy low risk patients to annual visits reduce unnecessary demand
- Identify patients who damage culture don't invite them back crisis creates permission to improve mix
- Set aggressive goals with two to three month timelines not quarterly keeps team focused daily
- Leave strategic capacity blocks open for high value treatment even if creates short term anxiety
- Train front office to seek right treatment for priority blocks versus filling with whatever comes
- Implement pre appointment fees if needed economic friction reduces demand from price sensitive low value patients
- Drop low value insurance networks if capacity constrained reduces patient volume improves revenue per visit
- Use assisted hygiene for records and photos tighten hygienist schedule increase throughput without adding chairs
- Create monthly bonus structures instead of quarterly immediate timelines drive consistent daily focus and behavior
- Enforce confirmation protocols show patients texts and calls when they arrive without confirming trains accountability
- Review reappoint conversations stop saying cancel anytime creates no pressure which guarantees future no shows
- Build systems that hold fundamentals during crisis bending is acceptable breaking abandoning core systems is not
