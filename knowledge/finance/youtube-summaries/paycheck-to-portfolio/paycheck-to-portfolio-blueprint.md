# Paycheck to Portfolio Blueprint

## Turning W-2 Income into Monthly Dividend Income

Hello, I'm Shawn, a husband, father of four, and someone who once believed financial freedom was reserved for retirement at 65. Now, I'm building it today through my brokerage account. This guide shares my strategy for reinvesting every paycheck into income-producing assets, not for some distant future, but right now. My goal is to live with purpose, flexibility, and faith-driven stewardship.

---

## My Strategy: High-Yield + Growth Hybrid

Each paycheck follows a clear path: I transfer funds directly to my E\*TRADE brokerage account and invest in a mix of high-yield and growth assets. Here's how it breaks down:

### High-Yield Core (Target Yield: 27%+)

**Investments:** CLM, CRF, GOF, QQQY, SPYI, QQQI, YMAX, MSTY, AMZY, and similar funds.

**DRIP Strategy:** Reinvest dividends at Net Asset Value (NAV) for CLM, CRF, and GOF (GOF at a 5% NAV discount). Only certain brokerages, like E\*TRADE and Fidelity, offer DRIP at NAV.

**Non-DRIP Dividends:** Used to cover monthly bills and margin interest.

### Growth Positions

**Investments:** SPYG, MCD, COST, and similar stocks.

**DRIP Strategy:** Dividends are reinvested to compound long-term growth.

### Margin Strategy

**Equity Maintenance:** I keep equity above 50%.

**Usage:** Margin is used only for living expenses and to bridge dividend income gaps.

**Rule:** Never use margin for speculative investments.

---

## The Paycheck to Portfolio Path

Every paycheck follows this cycle:

1. **W-2 Income → Brokerage Account**
2. **Invest in Dividend/Growth Assets → DRIP + Monthly Income**
3. **Use Margin for bills and flexibility, keeping usage under 50%**
4. **Repeat with every paycheck**

As Albert Einstein said, "Compounding is the eighth wonder of the world. He who understands it, earns it... he who doesn't, pays it." The key isn't perfection, it's repetition.

## What This Strategy Achieves

Here's a snapshot of my portfolio and goals starting in 2025:

- **Starting Net Account Value:** $103,000 (Gross is $200,000)
- **Target Annual Growth:** 16–18% (from dividends, reinvested equity, and margin leverage)
- **Annual Dividend Income:** $39,000 initially, growing to $100,000+ in 5 years
- **Net Equity Goal:** $1.6 million by 2029
- **Margin Usage:** Kept at or below 49% (subject to change based on market conditions, but always prudent)

If I stay consistent, my portfolio will outpace my cost of debt, allowing me to fully replace my W-2 income by 2027–2028.

---

## How I Stay on Track

- **Weekly Monitoring:** I track income versus expenses.
- **DRIP at NAV:** I reinvest dividends only at NAV for key assets.
- **Hedging:** I use low-cost puts on SPY or QQQ monthly to manage risk.
- **Equity Safety:** I never let equity drop below 50%.
- **Multiple Income Streams:** I diversify with real estate, dividends, margin, and options. If margin utilization creeps up above 50%, I will let my paycheck reduce this utilization.
